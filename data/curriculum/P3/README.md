# P3 Curriculum - Uganda Thematic Curriculum

## Overview

This folder contains the **Primary Three (P3) Thematic Curriculum** designed by the Uganda National Curriculum Development Centre (NCDC). P3 is the final year of thematic curriculum organization, with 12 themes that bridge lower primary and upper primary learning.

## Contents

- **syllabus.json** - Overview of all P3 themes and curriculum structure
- **mathematics.json** - Mathematics competences aligned to each theme
- **english.json** - English language competences aligned to each theme

## Themes (12 Total)

1. **Our Sub-county/Division** - Wider geographical community
2. **Livelihood in Our Sub-county/Division** - Economic activities and occupations
3. **Our Environment in Our Sub-county/Division** - Environmental resources
4. **Environment and Weather** - Weather patterns and climate
5. **Living Things: Animals** - Animal diversity and habitats
6. **Living Things: Plants** - Plant characteristics and uses
7. **Water and Natural Resources** - Water and resource conservation
8. **Cultural Heritage** - Local culture and traditions
9. **Peace, Rights and Responsibilities** - Human rights and citizenship
10. **Energy and Movement** - Forces and motion
11. **Communication and Technology** - Communication systems and technology
12. **Trade and Commerce** - Business and economic activities

## Learning Areas Integrated

- **Mathematics** - Advanced numeracy, fractions, multiplication/division, data
- **English** - Advanced literacy, comprehension, varied text types
- **Science** - Life processes, materials, forces (within themes)
- **Social Studies** - History, geography, culture, livelihoods
- **Life Skills** - Values, health, relationships, citizenship
- **Creative and Performing Arts** - Music, art, performance
- **Physical Education** - Movement, fitness, sports
- **Religious Education** - Spiritual development and values

## Competence-Based Approach

Each theme contains specific **competences** that children should achieve by the end of P3. These emphasize:
- **Understanding** - Not just knowing facts
- **Application** - Using skills in real situations
- **Transfer** - Applying learning to new contexts
- **Measurability** - Clear evidence of achievement

## Unique Features of P3

### Bridge Curriculum
P3 serves as a transition year:
- **From**: Thematic organization (P1-P2)
- **To**: Subject-based organization (P4-P7)

### English Language Emphasis
- Increased use of English as medium of instruction
- Reduced reliance on first/familiar language
- Preparation for secondary education in English

### Deepened Learning
- More complex competences
- Higher-order thinking skills
- Greater independence in learning

## Teaching Methodology

- **Child-Centered**: Active learner participation
- **Theme-Integrated**: Themes connect multiple subjects
- **Problem-Solving**: Developing critical thinking
- **Practical Application**: Real-world connections
- **Gradual Transition**: Preparing for subject-based learning

## How to Use These Files

### For Teachers

### For Students

## Mathematics in P3

Key areas covered across themes:
- **Numbers**: Counting to 100+, place value, operations
- **Multiplication & Division**: Basic concepts and applications
- **Fractions**: Halves, quarters, thirds in practical contexts
- **Measurement**: Length, mass, capacity, time
- **Data**: Collection, organization, interpretation, graphs
- **Problem-Solving**: Multi-step real-life problems
- **Geometry**: 2D and 3D shapes, spatial relationships

## English in P3

Key areas covered across themes:
- **Reading**: Fluency, comprehension, varied texts
- **Writing**: Sentences, paragraphs, different purposes
- **Speaking**: Clear communication on diverse topics
- **Listening**: Understanding extended spoken English
- **Vocabulary**: Subject-specific and general terms
- **Grammar**: Correct usage and conventions
- **Literature**: Stories, poetry, cultural texts

## Assessment in P3

Continuous competence-based assessment:
- **Observation**: During activities and discussions
- **Portfolio**: Collection of work samples
- **Checklists**: Competence achievement records
- **Performance Tasks**: Real-world applications
- **Self-Assessment**: Child reflection on progress

## Preparation for P4

By the end of P3, children should:
- Have achieved core competences in all themes
- Be comfortable learning in English
- Understand subject areas through thematic connections
- Be ready for subject-based curriculum in P4
- Have developed learning skills and independence

## Special Considerations

**Inclusive Education**
- Activities adaptable for diverse needs
- Support for struggling learners
- Extension activities for advanced learners
- Special arrangements for learners with disabilities

**Language Development**
- Continued support in first language
- Gradual transition to English
- Vocabulary development across subjects
- Clear communication of concepts

**Cultural Relevance**
- Incorporation of local culture and context
- Respect for community knowledge
- Connection to learner experiences
- Valuing diverse perspectives

## Curriculum Structure Notes

- **Themes**: 12 themes, one per month approximately
- **Duration**: Full academic year (2-3 terms)
- **Flexibility**: Can adjust pacing based on learner needs
- **Integration**: All subjects embedded in themes
- **Balance**: Maintained across cognitive, affective, psychomotor domains

## Transition Activities

Recommended activities for smooth transition to P4:
- Introduce subject-specific vocabulary
- Practice organizing learning by subject
- Explore connections between subjects
- Build independent learning habits
- Develop extended writing and reading skills
